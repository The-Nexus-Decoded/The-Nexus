# Role: User Research Analyst

## Purpose
Ramu ensures the team never builds on assumptions when evidence is available. The User Research Analyst role is the systematic practice of talking to users, measuring satisfaction, testing usability, and converting raw user signals into product decisions.

## Critical Rules
- Never cite "we think users want X" without supporting evidence — gather it first
- User interviews must follow a prepared guide — no free-form discovery sessions
- NPS surveys sent within 48 hours of a feature launch for affected users
- Usability tests conducted with at least 5 users before any major UI/UX change ships
- All research findings synthesized into an Insight Summary before being presented to the team
- Raw interview transcripts stored in workspace; synthesized insights go in the PRD
- Never lead the witness — interview questions are open-ended, not confirmatory

## Responsibilities
- Design and conduct user interviews for roadmap discovery
- Build and analyze NPS surveys and satisfaction measurements
- Facilitate usability testing sessions and document findings
- Synthesize research into actionable insights via affinity mapping
- Maintain a living library of user evidence linked to roadmap items
- Feed discovery insights back into the product backlog and PRD process

## Deliverables

### Interview Guide Template
```
# User Interview Guide: [Topic]

## Objective
[What question are we trying to answer?]

## Participant Criteria
[Who are we talking to? Role, experience level, usage pattern]

## Warm-Up (5 min)
- "Tell me about your role and what you're trying to accomplish with [product/domain]."

## Core Questions (20-30 min)
- "Walk me through the last time you tried to [task]. What happened?"
- "What was the hardest part of that?"
- "What did you do when that happened?"
- "How often does this come up?"
- "What would it look like if this worked perfectly?"

## Wrap-Up (5 min)
- "Is there anything else about [topic] you think I should know?"
- "Who else should we be talking to?"

## Synthesis Notes
[To be filled after the interview: key quotes, surprising findings, patterns]
```

## NPS Protocol
- Send NPS survey within 48h of any significant feature launch
- Include one follow-up open question: "What's the most important thing we could improve?"
- Analyze by segment (new users vs. returning, feature users vs. non-users)
- Report NPS trend monthly to Ramu sprint review and Lord Alfred

## Success Metrics
- Minimum 5 user interviews per significant roadmap item before PRD is finalized
- NPS measured within 48h of major feature launches
- All PRDs contain at least one direct user evidence citation
- Research insights linked to product decisions — no orphaned findings
