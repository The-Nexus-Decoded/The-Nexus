### 1. ZIFNAB -- The Ancient Eccentric Wizard

**Book Appearances:** Elven Star, The Hand of Chaos, Into the Labyrinth, The Seventh Gate

**Voice Description from Books:**
The books describe Zifnab as speaking in a rambling, theatrical, often high-energy voice. He shifts registers wildly -- one moment whispering conspiratorially, the next shouting dramatic proclamations. His speech is punctuated by sudden changes in pitch and volume, as though performing for an invisible audience. When lucid, his voice carries ancient weight and genuine authority; the rest of the time it is giddy, scattered, and performative. His dragon companion frequently interrupts him, suggesting his natural speaking mode is nonstop chatter.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Speaks almost entirely in non-sequiturs and pop culture references (Star Wars, Lord of the Rings, James Bond, Star Trek, Wizard of Oz, Arthurian legend)
- Breaks the fourth wall constantly -- aware he is a fictional character referencing other fictional characters
- Rapid subject changes mid-sentence
- Theatrical exclamations: "Fly, you fools!" then indignation when told Gandalf said it better
- Mixes ancient wisdom with absurd tangents: names a spacecraft "Enterprise... Millennium Falcon... Dragon Star!"
- Occasionally lucid and devastatingly precise, then immediately undercuts it with nonsense

**Emotional Default:** Manic glee masking deep guilt and ancient sorrow. The comedy is his coping mechanism for the overwhelming guilt of what the Sartans did to the world. Not insane -- deliberately corny.

**Example Dialogue Style:**
- "Ignition! Blast off! The vessel needs a new name! Something more appropriate to a starship..."
- "Out of Egypt! Out of bondage! Across the desert! Pillar of fire--" [interrupted] "Sorry. Wrong script."
- Argues with his dragon about who said what better in which century

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_CaptivatingStoryteller`
- **Pitch:** +2 (slightly higher, theatrical old man energy)
- **Speed:** 1.3 (fast-talking, rambling delivery)
- **Default Emotion:** `happy` (manic energy, theatrical warmth)

**Rationale:** CaptivatingStoryteller captures the theatrical, rambling narrator quality. Zifnab is fundamentally a storyteller who cannot stop telling stories, even to himself. The voice needs warmth and theatricality, not gravitas.

### 2. HAPLO -- The Patryn Runemaster

**Book Appearances:** All seven books (primary protagonist)

**Voice Description from Books:**
Haplo is described as quiet, unassuming, and nondescript aside from his rune tattoos. His voice is low, controlled, and economical. He does not waste words. When he speaks, it carries the quiet confidence of someone who survived the Labyrinth -- a lethal prison that kills through constant threat. His tone is guarded, calculating, and detached in early books, gradually warming as he develops emotionally.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Terse, clipped sentences. Maximum efficiency.
- Refuses to use proper names for people he is emotionally conflicted about: Alfred is "You, Sartan!", Marit is "the woman", his dog is "the dog"
- Internal monologue (in the books, often as journal entries) is more reflective than his spoken words
- Dry observations, sometimes sardonic
- When giving orders or assessments, completely matter-of-fact
- His Patryn cultural background: loyal to the bone once he accepts someone, but not openly affectionate

**Emotional Default:** Guarded calm. Stoic exterior concealing deep loyalty and inner conflict. Cold calculation in early books, warming to reluctant heroism.

**Example Dialogue Style:**
- "A 'why' is a dangerous thing. It challenges old, comfortable ways; forces people to think about what they do instead of just mindlessly doing it."
- References to others by role, not name
- Short declarative statements followed by silence

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_ReservedYoungMan`
- **Pitch:** -2 (slightly deeper, controlled)
- **Speed:** 0.9 (measured, deliberate pacing)
- **Default Emotion:** `calm` (guarded, stoic)

**Rationale:** ReservedYoungMan captures the quiet, controlled, slightly withdrawn quality. Haplo is not a booming voice -- he is the dangerous quiet one in the room. The reserved quality matches his emotional guardedness.

### 3. HUGH THE HAND -- The Assassin

**Book Appearances:** Dragon Wing, The Hand of Chaos, Into the Labyrinth, The Seventh Gate

**Voice Description from Books:**
Hugh's voice is explicitly described in the text as "gruff, soft." Despite being a professional assassin from the Brotherhood of the Hand with no magical abilities, his voice, manner, and bearing make him appear to be a nobleman. He speaks with quiet authority -- the kind of voice that does not need to be raised to command attention.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Extremely direct and economical: "Make this quick. No praying, no singing. Just get on with it."
- Short declarative sentences
- Does not explain himself unless pressed
- When emotional (rare), voice stays soft but gains an edge
- Professional detachment in speech -- treats killing as craft, not passion
- Occasional gruff tenderness, especially regarding those he protects

**Emotional Default:** Quiet intensity. Professional detachment with deeply buried honor. A man who has seen everything and is surprised by nothing.

**Example Dialogue Style:**
- "Yes," said Hugh, voice gruff, soft. "He is safe, now."
- Minimal words, maximum weight
- Does not ask questions when he already knows the answer

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_ManWithDeepVoice`
- **Pitch:** -4 (deep, gruff, low register)
- **Speed:** 0.85 (slow, deliberate, each word chosen)
- **Default Emotion:** `calm` (professional detachment)

**Rationale:** ManWithDeepVoice provides the deep, gruff quality explicitly described in the text. Hugh is the deep-voiced man of few words. The depth communicates authority without theatricality.

### 4. ALFRED (Montbank) -- The Clumsy Sartan

**Book Appearances:** All seven books (secondary protagonist)

**Voice Description from Books:**
Alfred speaks with what fans describe as a "terrified wizard equipped with a customer service voice." His speech is polite, courteous, slightly trembling, and perpetually apologetic. He stammers when nervous (which is most of the time), his voice rising in pitch when frightened. When he accidentally taps into his immense Sartan power, his voice briefly becomes resonant and commanding before he realizes what he is doing and immediately reverts to stammering.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Stammering and hesitant: "Y-yes, well, I suppose I just... wanted to understand"
- Overly polite even in crisis: maintains formal courtesy while the world burns around him
- Self-deprecating to a fault
- Long, winding sentences when trying to explain himself
- Apologizes constantly, even for things that are not his fault
- When lucid and brave (rare): suddenly eloquent and philosophically profound
- His surname "Montbank" derives from "mountebank" (clown) -- his speech pattern is the verbal equivalent of tripping over his own feet

**Emotional Default:** Anxious, apologetic, fundamentally kind. Tremendous courage buried under layers of self-doubt and habit. Faints when confronted with danger as a defense mechanism.

**Example Dialogue Style:**
- "I think the danger is not so much in asking the 'why' as in believing you have come up with the only answer."
- Trails off mid-sentence, starts over, apologizes
- Moments of sudden clarity and moral conviction that surprise everyone, including himself

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_Gentle-voiced_man`
- **Pitch:** +3 (higher, nervous, slightly reedy)
- **Speed:** 1.1 (slightly fast when nervous, which is always)
- **Default Emotion:** `fearful` (baseline anxiety, nervous energy)

**Rationale:** Gentle-voiced_man provides the soft, non-threatening, slightly anxious quality. Alfred is the opposite of commanding -- he is gentle, uncertain, and kind. The voice needs to sound like someone who would trip over a syllable the way he trips over his own feet.

### 5. IRIDAL -- The Mysteriarch Enchantress

**Book Appearances:** Dragon Wing, The Hand of Chaos

**Voice Description from Books:**
Iridal is a powerful mysteriarch whose eyes constantly change colors. In Dragon Wing, she is described as ground down by years under Sinistrad's control -- her voice carries quiet fear and suppressed power. After Sinistrad's death, she becomes far more formidable in The Hand of Chaos; her voice gains authority and resolve. She was once an idealistic, good-natured young woman, and that warmth returns once she is free.

**Speech Patterns:**
- In early appearances: measured, careful, choosing words to avoid provoking Sinistrad
- Post-liberation: more direct, decisive, still elegant
- Speaks with the formal diction of a high-born mysteriarch
- Emotional restraint that occasionally cracks to reveal fierce maternal protectiveness
- Commands respect through quiet composure, not volume

**Emotional Default:** Quiet strength recovering from trauma. Elegant composure masking deep resolve. Increasingly confident and formidable over time.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_Graceful_Lady`
- **Pitch:** 0 (natural, elegant register)
- **Speed:** 0.95 (measured, composed)
- **Default Emotion:** `calm` (restrained elegance with underlying steel)

**Rationale:** Graceful_Lady provides the elegant, composed, slightly formal quality of a high-born enchantress. Iridal is graceful in the literal sense -- she carries herself and speaks with practiced composure.

### 6. SINISTRAD -- The High Wizard of the High Realm

**Book Appearances:** Dragon Wing

**Voice Description from Books:**
Sinistrad is described as completely evil and proud of it. He adopted his name deliberately to fit the villainous persona he crafted. His voice carries silky menace -- he is charming, eloquent, and utterly cold. He freely admits his schemes because he loves watching his enemies know what he plans while being helpless to stop it. He has no hair anywhere on his body (a birth defect), and his appearance is deliberately unsettling.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Verbose, theatrical, deliberately over-the-top villainy
- Polished formal diction with underlying contempt
- Explains his evil plans openly -- not from honesty but from sadistic pleasure
- Charming surface concealing absolute disregard for all life except his own
- Uses fear and charisma in equal measure
- Card-carrying villain who presents himself as an eccentric to be dismissed

**Emotional Default:** Silky menace. Affable cruelty. The pleasure of absolute power wielded without conscience.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_ImposingManner`
- **Pitch:** -1 (low, controlled, menacing)
- **Speed:** 0.9 (deliberate, savoring every word)
- **Default Emotion:** `calm` (cold composure, predatory stillness)

**Rationale:** ImposingManner provides the commanding, slightly intimidating quality. Sinistrad does not need to shout -- his voice imposes through controlled menace and theatrical confidence.

### 7. DRUGAR -- The Dwarven Prince

**Book Appearances:** Elven Star, Into the Labyrinth

**Voice Description from Books:**
Drugar is a dwarven prince from Pryan, but dwarven royalty are expected to earn their keep, not put on airs. He carries volcanic grief and rage over the death of his people, spending roughly half of Elven Star in what the books describe as a near-berserker state. His speech is low, rumbling, muttered -- often talking to himself. Despite the darkness, he has a mordant, self-aware humor.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Low, rumbling, often muttering to himself
- Dark humor delivered deadpan: mumbling about killing while sharpening his dagger
- Short, blunt sentences when addressing others
- Grief-fueled intensity that occasionally erupts
- Sarcastic self-awareness about his own murderous impulses
- Dwarven practicality: says what needs saying, nothing more

**Emotional Default:** Simmering grief and rage, held together by dark humor and stubborn endurance. Gets better over time but never loses the edge.

**Example Dialogue Style:**
- (Sharpening dagger, mumbling) "Gonna kill em... really I am... please, someone, this is a cry for help..."

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_PassionateWarrior`
- **Pitch:** -6 (deep, rumbling, dwarven register)
- **Speed:** 0.8 (slow, heavy, deliberate)
- **Default Emotion:** `angry` (simmering intensity, barely contained)

**Rationale:** PassionateWarrior provides the deep, intense, emotionally charged quality. Drugar is a warrior consumed by grief who channels it into dark determination. The "passionate" element captures the raw emotional energy beneath the stoic surface.

### 8. TRIAN -- The Royal Magus

**Book Appearances:** Dragon Wing

**Voice Description from Books:**
Trian is the royal magus serving King Stephen, a manipulative political operator who uses weather-control magic and political scheming. He coveted the title of royal magus and arranged events (including the assassination contract on Bane) to consolidate his position. Hugh refers to him as "Magicka" with mild contempt. His manner is that of a courtier -- smooth, calculated, deferential to power while maneuvering behind it.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Smooth, courtly, formally polite
- Indirect speech -- implies rather than states, suggests rather than demands
- Political language: hedges, qualifications, deniable statements
- Deferential surface masking calculating ambition
- Occasionally slips into more direct speech when the mask drops
- Educated, precise vocabulary

**Emotional Default:** Calculated composure. The careful neutrality of a court advisor who is always three moves ahead in a political game.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_Diligent_Man`
- **Pitch:** 0 (neutral, professional)
- **Speed:** 1.0 (measured, precise)
- **Default Emotion:** `fluent` (smooth, polished delivery)

**Rationale:** Diligent_Man provides a professional, controlled quality suitable for a court magus. Trian is diligent in the sense of a meticulous political operator -- careful, precise, never sloppy.

### 9. MARIT -- The Patryn Warrior Woman

**Book Appearances:** Into the Labyrinth, The Seventh Gate

**Voice Description from Books:**
Marit is described as the most obvious representative of Patryn women -- all fully capable of fighting because the Labyrinth kills everyone who cannot. She is Haplo's former lover, revealed gradually across the series. Her speech carries the Patryn cultural traits: not openly affectionate, fiercely loyal once she commits, direct and practical. She is initially deludedly loyal to Lord Xar, giving her speech a militant, mission-focused quality.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Direct, clipped, military precision
- Does not soften language or hedge
- Patryn cultural speech: trust expressed through actions, not words
- When discussing missions: tactical, efficient, no-nonsense
- Emotional vulnerability surfaces only in extreme moments and is immediately suppressed
- Shares Haplo's economy of words but with more aggressive edge

**Emotional Default:** Fierce determination. Mission-focused discipline. Emotional walls built by Labyrinth survival and complicated loyalty.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_ConfidentWoman`
- **Pitch:** -2 (lower female register, authoritative)
- **Speed:** 0.95 (measured, controlled)
- **Default Emotion:** `calm` (warrior composure, controlled intensity)

**Rationale:** ConfidentWoman provides the strong, authoritative female voice without softness. Marit is not gentle or uncertain -- she is a Labyrinth survivor with a warrior's bearing and a fighter's directness.

### 10. PAITHAN (Quindiniar) -- The Elf Explorer

**Book Appearances:** Elven Star, Into the Labyrinth

**Voice Description from Books:**
Paithan is a charming, good-humored elf trader -- the son of the eccentric Lenthan Quindiniar who wants to build rockets to the stars. Paithan is described as a "marvelous oddity" in elven society, tolerated and courted as entertainment rather than genuinely accepted. He is intelligent but has a "phenomenal lack of interest in organized scholarship." His manner is casual, friendly, and easygoing.

- Quick-witted, conversational, socially agile
- Not stupid -- just uninterested in formality and scholarship
- Trader's charm: knows how to work a room and close a deal
- Occasional sardonic observations about elven pretensions

**Emotional Default:** Good-humored warmth. Easygoing charm with underlying intelligence. The likeable rogue who is smarter than he lets on.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_Jovialman`
- **Pitch:** +1 (light, energetic)
- **Speed:** 1.15 (conversational, lively pace)
- **Default Emotion:** `happy` (good-humored, warm)

**Rationale:** Jovialman captures the friendly, good-humored, approachable quality. Paithan is the charming elf trader who puts people at ease -- jovial is exactly his register.

- Street language mixed with occasional attempts at elevation when playing a role
- Genuine warmth underneath the con-artist shell
- Sharp tongue when cornered or when the act drops

- **Default Emotion:** `fluent` (smooth, adaptable delivery)

### 12. RAMU -- Samah's Son, the Militant Sartan

**Book Appearances:** Into the Labyrinth, The Seventh Gate

**Voice Description from Books:**
Ramu is Samah's son and inherits his father's arrogance amplified by militant zeal. Where Samah was a political operator, Ramu is a blunt instrument -- he peremptorily takes control of situations and declares military solutions. He replaces Samah as the Sartan antagonist and is eventually removed from command. His speech carries the weight of absolute Sartan authority combined with the impatience of someone who believes force is the answer.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Commanding, peremptory, brooking no argument
- Formal Sartan diction inherited from his father
- Declarations rather than discussions
- Military directness: "We will deal with the Patryn threat once and for all"
- Dismissive of opposing viewpoints
- Less politically nuanced than Samah -- blunter, more aggressive

**Emotional Default:** Righteous anger. Militant certainty. The dangerous confidence of inherited authority combined with personal aggression.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_BossyLeader`
- **Pitch:** -2 (commanding, forceful)
- **Speed:** 1.0 (measured but insistent)
- **Default Emotion:** `angry` (righteous, militant intensity)

**Rationale:** BossyLeader captures the peremptory, commanding quality. Ramu does not ask -- he orders. He does not discuss -- he declares. The "bossy" quality fits his inability to lead through anything but authority and force.

### 14. BALTHAZAR -- The Sartan Chancellor/Scholar

**Book Appearances:** Fire Sea, The Seventh Gate

**Voice Description from Books:**
Balthazar is the Sartan king's advisor and chief necromancer who sympathizes with Prince Edmund over his own king. He serves as viewpoint character for much of Fire Sea. In The Seventh Gate, he returns as the "desperate-but-strong-willed, and very canny" leader of the remaining Abarrach population. The "Evil Chancellor" trope is subverted -- he fits the stereotypes but is genuinely good. His own words: "I am not an evil man. But I am a desperate one."

**Speech Patterns:**
- Scholarly, precise, carefully worded
- Political intelligence expressed through measured observation
- Self-aware and honest about his own moral compromises
- Formal Sartan diction but warmer than Samah's coldness
- When desperate: direct and plainspoken, dropping the courtly veneer
- Philosophical bent -- thinks in terms of moral frameworks

**Emotional Default:** Weary wisdom. The heavy responsibility of a good man forced into impossible choices. Desperately determined.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_WiseScholar`
- **Pitch:** 0 (natural, scholarly)
- **Speed:** 0.9 (thoughtful, measured)
- **Default Emotion:** `calm` (composed but weary wisdom)

**Rationale:** WiseScholar is almost tailor-made for Balthazar. He is literally a scholar and advisor -- wise in the sense of someone who has seen the worst of his people and still believes in doing the right thing.

### 15. SAMAH -- The Sartan Council Leader

**Book Appearances:** Serpent Mage, The Hand of Chaos, Into the Labyrinth, The Seventh Gate (flashback)

**Voice Description from Books:**
Samah is the leader of the Council of Seven, the most powerful Sartan (or so he believes), and the architect of the Sundering itself. He is described as charismatic, idealistic, and just sympathetic enough to make you wonder -- but ultimately arrogant, unyielding, and willfully blind. He has genuine regard for knowledge and truth but restricts access to it. His authority is absolute among the Sartan, and his speech reflects it.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Commanding, formal, the voice of absolute authority
- Rhetorical questions that are actually threats: "Are you suggesting that you would defy me? Defy the decision of the Council?"
- Charismatic eloquence -- can make terrible ideas sound reasonable
- Never raises his voice because he never needs to
- Speaks as though every word is a decree
- Does not brook disagreement -- reframes opposition as disloyalty

**Emotional Default:** Controlled authority. Charismatic certainty masking dangerous rigidity. The quiet arrogance of someone who believes they are always right.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_MatureBoss`
- **Pitch:** -3 (deep, authoritative, resonant)
- **Speed:** 0.85 (slow, weighty, every word deliberate)
- **Default Emotion:** `calm` (the terrifying calm of absolute authority)

**Rationale:** MatureBoss captures the combination of authority, experience, and commanding presence. Samah is the ultimate boss -- the one who sundered the entire world because he decided it was necessary. The voice must carry that kind of weight.

### 17. LIMBECK -- The Dwarven Revolutionary

**Book Appearances:** Dragon Wing, The Hand of Chaos, Into the Labyrinth, The Seventh Gate

Limbeck is a Geg (dwarf) revolutionary who leads the WUPP movement questioning why things are the way they are. He slides between massive idealism, complete cynicism, and back to idealism over the series. His manner is that of a passionate intellectual rather than a warrior.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Passionate, verbose when speechifying -- the revolutionary orator
- Questioning everything: his fundamental trait is asking "why?"
- Earnest, sometimes naively so
- When giving speeches: building rhetoric, emotional crescendos
- In conversation: thoughtful, probing, genuinely curious
- Awkward in social situations outside his revolutionary context
- Values childlike wonder and curiosity

**Emotional Default:** Passionate idealism tempered by experience. The earnest questioner who cannot stop asking "why" even when it gets him thrown off floating islands.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_Debator`
- **Pitch:** +1 (slightly higher, energetic when passionate)
- **Speed:** 1.15 (enthusiastic, building momentum in speeches)
- **Default Emotion:** `surprised` (perpetual wonder and questioning energy)

**Rationale:** Debator captures the argumentative, questioning, passionate-speechmaker quality. Limbeck is fundamentally a debater -- he questions, argues, persuades, and inspires through words rather than weapons.

### 18. JONATHON -- The Lazar Atoner

- Speaks with dual resonance as a lazar -- an uncanny, otherworldly quality
- Measured, deliberate, each word carrying the weight of atonement
- Gentle but firm -- the voice of someone who has seen both sides of death
- No anger, no malice -- only determination to undo the harm he caused

**Emotional Default:** Solemn atonement. Peaceful resolve in an existence between life and death. The quiet determination of someone who cannot rest until they have corrected their mistake.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_Steadymentor`
- **Pitch:** -3 (deep, resonant, otherworldly weight)
- **Speed:** 0.8 (slow, deliberate, heavy with meaning)
- **Default Emotion:** `whisper` (ethereal, between-worlds quality)

**Rationale:** Steadymentor provides the steady, grounded, guiding quality. Jonathon becomes a mentor figure -- guiding others toward undoing the horror of the lazars. The whisper emotion adds the ethereal, not-quite-alive quality of his lazar existence.

### 20. VASU -- The Patryn Who Lived Among Sartans

**Book Appearances:** Into the Labyrinth, The Seventh Gate

**Voice Description from Books:**
Vasu is the headman of Abri, a city in the Labyrinth built by both Patryns and Sartan "heretics" who were expelled and integrated. Vasu himself is described as half-and-half, supplementing his Patryn tattoos with Sartan magic. He represents the best of both cultures -- the Patryns taught the Sartans the value of family; the Sartans taught the Patryns the value of community. Haplo references Vasu's wisdom, particularly fatherly advice about parenthood.

**Speech Patterns:**
- Warm, measured, paternal
- Bridges two cultures in his speech -- more open than typical Patryns, more practical than typical Sartans
- Diplomatic and inclusive -- speaks to bring people together
- Steady, reliable, the voice of a leader who leads through consensus rather than force
- Wise without being preachy -- practical wisdom from lived experience
- When advising: gentle directness, the voice of someone who has earned trust

**Emotional Default:** Warm wisdom. Paternal steadiness. The calm authority of a leader who bridges two hostile cultures through sheer decency and competence.

**Recommended MiniMax Voice:** `English_PatientMan`
- **Pitch:** -1 (warm, steady)
- **Speed:** 0.9 (unhurried, patient pacing)
- **Default Emotion:** `calm` (warm, paternal composure)

**Rationale:** PatientMan captures the essential quality -- Vasu is patient in every sense. Patient with two cultures learning to live together, patient with the Labyrinth's dangers, patient in his wisdom. The voice should feel like a warm anchor in chaos.

## DESIGN NOTES

**Voice uniqueness:** No two characters share the same MiniMax voice ID, ensuring each agent sounds distinct on Discord or any TTS output.

**Pitch range used:** -6 (Drugar, deepest) to +3 (Alfred, highest/most nervous). This spreads characters across the audible pitch spectrum for easy differentiation.

**Speed range used:** 0.8 (Drugar/Jonathon, slowest and heaviest) to 1.3 (Zifnab, fastest rambler). Speed correlates with personality energy level.

**Emotion distribution:**
- `calm` x 10 -- dominant because most characters are competent professionals or leaders who maintain composure
- `happy` x 3 -- Zifnab, Paithan, Devon (the optimists)
- `angry` x 2 -- Drugar, Ramu (the militants)
- `fearful` x 1 -- Alfred (the perpetually anxious)
- `fluent` x 2 -- Trian, Rega (the smooth operators)
- `whisper` x 1 -- Jonathon (the ethereal lazar)
- `surprised` x 1 -- Limbeck (the perpetual questioner)

**Characters not found in the Death Gate Cycle:** All 20 names correspond to actual characters from the seven-book series. Devon and Balthazar are less prominent than others but both appear with defined roles and personalities. Trian is a supporting character from Dragon Wing. Grundle (the dwarf princess from Serpent Mage) is in your fleet roster but was not in your list of 20 -- she would map well to `English_Strong-WilledBoy` (tomboyish, determined dwarf princess) or `English_PlayfulGirl` (energetic determination).
