# Soul Drifter — Narrative Framework

## The World

The Death Gate Cycle ended with the Death Gate destroyed, the realms severed, and the Sartan scattered. Centuries have passed. The elemental worlds are dying.

- **Arianus (Sky)** — The sky is falling. Islands drift into the void. Wind no longer answers the Aerians.
- **Chelestra (Water)** — The great sea is draining. The Durnai survive on shrinking floating platforms.
- **Pryan (Fire)** — The eternal flame extinguished. The Sartan sleep in cold ashes.
- **Abarrach (Death)** — The Labyrinth itself is collapsing. Souls trapped for millennia are breaking free.

The Death Gate is gone. The only way between realms is the **Soul Drift** — the dangerous practice of binding fragments of dead Sartan/Patryn power to fly between dying worlds.

---

## The Villain — The Naga

**Origin:** The Naga were servants in the Labyrinth — humans mutated by centuries of exposure to Patryn runes and the void between worlds. When the Death Gate shattered, they were forgotten. They evolved.

**Goal:** To tear down what remains and rebuild from the void. They worship **Entropy** itself — not destruction for its own sake, but *ending* to make way for *new beginning*.

**The Naga Caste:**
- **Void Walkers** — Assassins that phase through matter
- **Corruptors** — Spread entropy decay to realms
- **The Hiss** — Collective consciousness, speaks in unison
- **The Seven Fold** — Naga lords, each representing a dead sin

**Corruption mechanic:** Naga corruption spreads like rot through the realms. Players must cleanse it with soul fragments.

---

## The Player — Soul Drifter

You are a **Drifter** — one of the few who can bind soul fragments and navigate the void between dying worlds.

**Your origin:** You are human (or human-descended). The high races (Sartan, Patryn) are legends — godlike beings who trapped themselves in their own creation.

**Your purpose:** Collect soul fragments, restore the realms, prevent Naga corruption from consuming what remains.

**Your power:** Bind souls = gain abilities. More fragments = more power. Risk: too many fragments = losing yourself to the dead.

## Phase Progression

### Phase 1: Arianus-Sky (Zone 1 Demo)

**Realm:** Arianus, the dying sky
**Threat:** Windshear Stalkers, Thermal Wraiths, early Naga corruption
**Goal:** Restore wind flow, reach the first Sky Anchor

**Lore hooks:**
- "The sky remembers when it carried us. Now it carries nothing."
- "They chose to stay. The sky chose to fall."

**Narrative arc:**
1. Enter Arianus → discover dying world
2. Find Thermal Core → learn about the Sundering
3. First combat → understand the threat
4. Post-combat lore → what was lost
5. Reach Zone Gate → "Above, the air still holds warmth"

---

### Phase 2: Chelestra-Deep (Water Realm)

**Realm:** Chelestra, the draining ocean
**Threat:** Naga Corruptors spreading decay, Durnai under siege
**Goal:** Restore water flow, unite Durnai survivors

**New mechanics:**
- Underwater navigation
- Soul fragments from drowned Sartan
- Durnai faction reputation

---

### Phase 3: Pryan-Ash (Fire Realm)

**Realm:** Pryan, the cold fire world
**Threat:** Eternal flame extinguished, Sartan sleeping in ash
**Goal:** Rekindle the flame, awaken the Sartan

**New mechanics:**
- Thermal puzzles
- Sartan rune magic (learned, not given)
- Patryn encounter (tense alliance)

---

### Phase 4: Abarrach-Void (Death Realm)

**Realm:** Abarrach, the collapsed Labyrinth
**Threat:** The Hiss spreading from the void, trapped souls breaking loose
**Goal:** Seal the void breach, free trapped Patryn

**New mechanics:**
- Reality instability (walls shift)
- Soul fragment overload risk
- Face the Patryn king — alliance or battle?

---

### Phase 5: The Labyrinth (Finale)

**Realm:** The Nexus, center of the Labyrinth
**Threat:** The Seven Fold, Naga leadership
**Goal:** Destroy the Naga, restore the Death Gate, reunite the realms

**Final choice:** 
- Restore Death Gate = realms separate but stable
- Destroy Naga = realms merge (chaos, new world)
- Player decision shapes ending

---

## Tone

**Core feeling:** Hope in decay. The world is dying, but it *can* be saved.

**Emotional palette:**
- Wonder at dying beauty
- Triumph in small victories  
- Sadness for what's lost
- Determination to try anyway

**Not:** Grimdark nihilism. This is not "everything is doomed." This is "everything is doomed *unless*."

---

## Key Themes

1. **Collective vs. Individual** — Binding souls: power at cost of self
2. **Legacy** — What remains when gods fail
3. **Adaptation** — Humans survive where gods failed
4. **Balance** — The Wave returns — equilibrium or extinction
